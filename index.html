Paisa.tech is a mobile banking experience that is simple, fast, secure, and easy to use.

It's 2017. Banking on your phone shouldn't be this hard. There's all sorts of problems with banking applications. In the name of having an easy to use interface, the apps have custom UIs that largely ignore platform conventions, and end up with confusing interactions.


A lot of major banks have many single purpose apps — one for checking your balance, one for funds transfer, one for wallets/virtual card transactions. Based on an informal survey, I identified 4 major things people want to get done with their banking apps: checking balance, checking recent transactions, sending payments and paying bills. A bank app should ideally get all of these done in a single app.

It’s understandable that banks are paranoid about security, there are ways to keep the app secure without having two different types of passwords, OTP verification, and security questions just to log in. There’s also the question of just how secure OTP verification really is — Tony Chew, Citibank’s global head of cybersecurity regulatory strategy said at a conference in 2016 that it was “absurd” that banks today still rely on passwords and PINs to manage user access.
